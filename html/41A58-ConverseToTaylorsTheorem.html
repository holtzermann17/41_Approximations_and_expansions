converse to Taylor’s theorem


Let U⊂ℝn be an open set.

Theorem.

Let f:U→R be a function such that there exists a constant C>0 and an integer k≥0 such that for each x∈U there is a polynomial px⁢(y) of k where

|f⁢(x+y)-px⁢(y)|≤C⁢|y|k+1

for y near 0. Then f∈Ck⁢(U) (f is k continuously differentiable) and the Taylor expansion (http://planetmath.org/TaylorSeries) of k of f about any x∈U is given by px.

Note that when k=0 the hypothesis of the theorem is just that f is Lipschitz in U which certainly makes it continuous in U.
