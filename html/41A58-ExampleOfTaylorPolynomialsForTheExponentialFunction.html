example of Taylor polynomials for the exponential function


Example 1.

We construct the nth Taylor polynomial for f⁢(x)=ex around x=0. As we know all derivatives of ex equal ex and also, e0=1. Therefore, f(n)⁢(0)=1 for any n. Thus:

T1⁢(x) = 1+x
T2⁢(x) = 1+x+x22
T3⁢(x) = 1+x+x22+x33!=1+x+x22+x36
T4⁢(x) = 1+x+x22+x33!+x44!=1+x+x22+x36+x424

In fact:

Tn⁢(x)=1+x+x22+x33!+x44!+…+xnn!

Comparison of ex with the Taylor pol. of deg. 1 (green), 2 (blue) and 3 (pink).

Let us use several Taylor polynomials to find approximations of the number e:

e = 2.718281828459045⁢…
e≈T1⁢(1) = 1+1=2
e≈T2⁢(1) = 1+1+1/2=2.5
e≈T3⁢(1) = 1+1+1/2+1/6=8/3=2.666⁢6¯
e≈T4⁢(1) = 1+1+1/2+1/6+1/24=65/24=2.708333⁢3¯
e≈T5⁢(1) = 1+1+1/2+1/6+1/24+1/120=163/60=2.71666⁢6¯
